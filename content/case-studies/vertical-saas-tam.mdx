## The Situation

A construction management SaaS platform at Series B had been targeting large general contractors for 3 years. Their defined ICP was "general contractors over $100M revenue" — a universe of only 2,000 companies nationally.

Penetration in this segment was already at 12% and growth was slowing. The team suspected there was more market, but had no data model to prove it or act on it.

## What We Built

BMA ran a 60-day TAM redefinition project, then a 90-day GTM build to activate the new segments.

The approach centered on building a permit-data driven model of contractor activity. By sourcing building permit records from all 50 states, we identified 340,000 active contractors by activity level — a 170x larger raw universe than their original ICP.

We then layered contractor licensing data, technology adoption signals, and win/loss patterns from their existing customer base to identify the 74,000 accounts with real conversion potential.

## The Results

The market expansion unlocked $4.2M in new ARR in the first six months. The specialty contractor segment actually outperformed their traditional enterprise segment on win rate, which reshaped how they allocated sales capacity.

Their original $100M+ GC focus became just one of six distinct buyer personas, each with tailored sequencing and value propositions.
